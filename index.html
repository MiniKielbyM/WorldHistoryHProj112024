Type of Government

The Shogun was the military dictator who held the real power, while the Emperor was a figurehead.

Local lords, known as Daimyo, controlled their own territories and owed allegiance to the Shogun.

General Information

Feudal Japan was characterized by a decentralized political structure where local lords held significant power.

The feudal system was established in the 12th century and lasted until the 19th century.

Rights and Freedoms

Samurai had significant privileges, including the right to bear arms and a stipend from their lords.

Peasants, artisans, and merchants had fewer rights and were subject to the authority of the Samurai and Daimyo.

Economy

The economy was primarily agrarian, with rice being the main crop and currency.

Trade and commerce were also important, with merchants playing a key role in the distribution of goods.

Values

Honor, loyalty, and duty were central values in feudal Japan, especially among the Samurai class.

These values were embodied in the Bushido code, which guided the behavior of the Samurai.

Pitfalls

The feudal system was marked by social inequality and limited social mobility.

Conflicts between Daimyo and Samurai, as well as natural disasters, also posed challenges to the stability of the system.
